Working at Rouse's Supermarket 3/2015-7/2016

I started at Rouse’s as a cashier on the front end. It didn’t take long for me to get good at ringing up customers as quickly as possible. In under two months, I had one of the highest RPMs (rings per minute) of all the cashiers at the store. I’d memorized most of the produce codes and the layout of the store. I had the added responsibilities of taking phone calls, managing cashier/bagger breaks and duties, performing advanced operations on the POS system (i.e. returns, discounts, specialty items), opening/closing out tills, and balancing the “bank” at the end of my shift. While I may not have been in the position for long (5 months), it was the first time I was able to develop my leadership abilities.

Working at Raising Cane's 2/2017-Current

The Raising Cane’s I work at (C1 on LSU campus) is a fast-paced and high stress environment that requires every employee to communicate well with one another. There are a lot of customers that come in daily, and even more so when LSU football has a home game. The only aspect of this job that was new to me when I started was the food service. Once I was able to get that down, I quickly became a Certified Trainer. This requires you to be cross-trained to work in the kitchen or at the counter, and to be good enough at both to teach somebody how to do it. I was able to fine-tune my leadership abilities as a trainer since I was a role model for all new hires (trainee or not). Since it’s the food industry, people are extremely picky about how their food comes out; if it’s not perfect, it’s most likely going to be sent back or a customer may leave a bad survey. This meant order accuracy had to be high and wait times had to be low or there’d be a lot of angry customers to deal with. When events like this do happen, I’m always able to assure the customer leaves satisfied. There are biannual evaluations to let you know if you’re improving or not, and each time including the first I’ve been in the highest category (no improvement needed).
